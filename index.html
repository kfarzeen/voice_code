Feature Visualizations

Each plot is a heatmap: the horizontal axis is time, the vertical axis is a feature index, and the colour shows how strong that feature is at that time. Brighter regions mean more energy / importance in that time–frequency area.

MFCC (Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients)

MFCCs capture the overall shape of the speech spectrum – how bright, muffled or nasal the voice sounds. They are strongly related to pronunciation patterns and therefore very important for dialect differences.

Chroma

Chroma features measure how much energy is present in each of the 12 pitch classes (C, C#, D, …). They reflect intonation and melody patterns of speech, which can vary across dialects.

Mel Spectrogram

The Mel spectrogram shows energy across frequency bands over time. It is a detailed time–frequency picture of the audio and helps the model capture how low and high frequency components evolve as the speaker talks.

Spectral Contrast

Spectral contrast measures the difference between peaks and valleys in the spectrum. It characterises how “sharp” or “flat” the sound is, which can differ between dialects due to articulation and emphasis.

Tonnetz

Tonnetz features describe harmonic relationships between pitches. They capture subtle rhythm and intonation cues (for example, rising vs. falling patterns) that also carry dialect-specific information.

LIME – Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations

LIME builds a simple, local model around this one audio example. It tells us which features increased or decreased the probability of the detected dialect. Positive weight means the feature made the dialect more likely; negative weight means it pushed the prediction away from that dialect.
